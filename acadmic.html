Academics

The department offers PhD, MPhil, MS (Information Science & Technology), M.Sc. and BS degrees. Each degree programme has a specific objective and focus. The course of study and syllabi for each degree is updated and aligns with its objectives.

PhD is the highest degree awarded in an academic discipline and is the most rewarding educational experience. Students work closely with a faculty member, performing original research and tackling challenging, unsolved problems. (More Details)

The objective of the MPhil Computer Science programme is to train students to initiate research work. The duration of the MPhil programme is two years (4 semesters). MPhil consists of 50 credit hours: 24 for coursework and 26 for research. Each course carries 3 credit hours. (More Details)

In today's Information Society, expertise in information systems is key for success in science, business, and education. The MS in Information Science & Technology (IST) aims to design and develop IT-based solutions by understanding the relationship between digital information, people, and technology.

The program integrates Computer Science and Information Technology with a collaborative focus on how people interact with digital systems.

Program Options:

  • MS (IST) Part Time - 6 credit hours per semester
  • M.Sc. - 2 years (Computer Science, Software Engineering, IT)

The BS Computer Science program at QAU provides a well-integrated balance of theoretical and practical knowledge required for developing reliable and usable software tools for different fields. The program enables students to design, implement and manage systems combining scientific, computational, communication and multimedia facilities in different domains. The main objective of the program is to provide students with a balanced breadth and depth of knowledge in Computer Science that enables them to begin their professional career in the software industry or they can continue their education at graduate level.
